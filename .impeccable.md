## Design Context

### Users
Students and career-switchers running cold outreach campaigns — primarily young people (undergrads, recent grads) trying to maximize internship or first-job chances. They're finding recruiters, writing email drafts, timing follow-ups. They use the app alongside Gmail, often during an anxious and repetitive job search process. They're less experienced with cold outreach and benefit from guidance, not just data.

### Brand Personality
**Confident · Warm · Focused** — feels like a smart friend helping you run your job search, not a corporate productivity tool. Should reduce anxiety, not add to it. The burnt orange accent and warm off-white palette already communicate this; every other design decision should reinforce it.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light mode only. Warm off-white base (`#f8f7f5`) with burnt orange accent (`#b85212`). Deliberately avoids the SaaS default of blue/indigo. Clean and dense enough for power use, warm enough to feel approachable during a stressful process.

**Current font stack:**
- **Syne** (display) — used only for the app title "Reach" and primary tab navigation; kept bold/geometric to anchor the brand
- **Plus Jakarta Sans** (body/sans) — humanist-leaning, warm, professional; replaced IBM Plex Sans which was too clinical for this audience
- **IBM Plex Mono** (mono) — dates, timestamps, numeric data only

**Anti-references:** Dark mode with glowing accents, generic blue SaaS, Inter/Roboto defaults, hero metric layouts, identical card grids.

### Design Principles
1. **Warm precision** — every element should feel intentional and human, not corporate or clinical
2. **Earned hierarchy** — use Syne sparingly so it means something; section labels don't need a display font
3. **Color communicates** — the burnt orange accent and status colors carry semantic meaning; don't dilute them with decorative use
4. **Reduce cognitive load** — students are already anxious; the UI should clarify, not overwhelm
5. **Distinctiveness through restraint** — the warm palette is the differentiator; keep it clean so the color does its job
